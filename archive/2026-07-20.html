01
LONGER
We should consider all options
Advise evaluating multiple alternatives before deciding.
Examples
"We should consider all options before finalizing the strategy."
"We should consider all options to mitigate risks."
Tone Tip
Use to encourage thorough decision-making.
02
SHORT
Per our last discussion
Reference a prior conversation or agreement.
Examples
"Per our last discussion, the deadline is moved to Friday."
"Per our last discussion, the client prefers Option A."
Tone Tip
Use to connect the current message to a previous context.
03
SHORT
Looking forward to your feedback
Express anticipation for a response or opinion.
Examples
"Looking forward to your feedback on the proposal."
"Looking forward to your feedback on the draft report."
Tone Tip
Use to politely request input after sending a document.
04
SHORT
Please submit the report by EOD
Request for timely submission of a report.
Examples
"Please submit the quarterly financial report by EOD."
"Kindly submit the project update by EOD today."
Tone Tip
Use for clear, concise deadlines in professional emails.
05
LONGER
Let's discuss this further in the meeting
Propose a follow-up discussion on a topic during a meeting.
Examples
"Let's discuss this further in the meeting to ensure clarity."
"Let's discuss this further in the meeting scheduled for tomorrow."
Tone Tip
Use to defer a complex topic to a dedicated discussion.

About This Program

A daily habit for building professional English vocabulary and phrasing — designed for non-native speakers working in English-language environments.

Five carefully selected phrases are generated every day at midnight. Each one is practical, work-relevant, and explained with real examples and tone guidance.

Phrase Categories

  • Meeting language — start, navigate, and close meetings
  • Email & report writing — professional written tone
  • Polite disagreement — push back without friction
  • Presenting ideas — structure and deliver arguments
  • Asking for clarification — professionally and confidently
  • Transitions & wrap-up — connect and conclude

How to Use It

Read each phrase in the morning or evening. Try to use at least one in your actual work that day — in a meeting, an email, or a report.

The best learning happens when you connect a phrase to a real situation you experienced.
