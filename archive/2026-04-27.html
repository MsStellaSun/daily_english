01
short fragment
wrapping up
to conclude a meeting or discussion
Examples
"Wrapping up the Q&A session; thanks for all the questions."
"Wrapping up this call; let's schedule the next one next week."
Tone Tip
Use to signal the end of a conversation or meeting in a polite manner.
02
short fragment
can't help wondering
to be unable to stop thinking about something, often a concern or question
Examples
"Can't help wondering if the Q2 projections will hold true."
"Can't help wondering about the implications of this decision."
Tone Tip
Use to express a lingering concern or thought in a polite, non-confrontational way.
03
short fragment
circle back on
to return to a previous topic or issue at a later time
Examples
"Let's circle back on the budget approval after we review the expenses."
"I'll circle back on your feedback regarding the presentation slides."
Tone Tip
Use to manage conversations and ensure important topics are revisited.
04
longer phrase
I see your point, but let's explore a different angle
to acknowledge someone's perspective while suggesting an alternative approach
Examples
"I see your point about the budget constraints, but let's explore a different angle by reducing operational costs."
"I see your point about the timeline, but let's explore a different angle by prioritizing key deliverables."
Tone Tip
Use to show respect for someone's opinion while proposing an alternative solution.
05
longer phrase
Let's ensure the strategic objectives are clearly articulated for all teams
to emphasize the importance of clear communication of goals
Examples
"Let's ensure the strategic objectives are clearly articulated for all teams to align our efforts."
"Let's ensure the strategic objectives are clearly articulated for all teams to avoid misalignment."
Tone Tip
Use to stress the need for clear and consistent communication of goals.

About This Program

A daily habit for building professional English vocabulary and phrasing — designed for non-native speakers working in English-language environments.

Five carefully selected phrases are generated every day at midnight. Each one is practical, work-relevant, and explained with real examples and tone guidance.

Phrase Categories

  • Meeting language — start, navigate, and close meetings
  • Email & report writing — professional written tone
  • Polite disagreement — push back without friction
  • Presenting ideas — structure and deliver arguments
  • Asking for clarification — professionally and confidently
  • Transitions & wrap-up — connect and conclude

How to Use It

Read each phrase in the morning or evening. Try to use at least one in your actual work that day — in a meeting, an email, or a report.

The best learning happens when you connect a phrase to a real situation you experienced.
